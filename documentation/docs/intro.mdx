# TEHS Document DB Guide

TEHS Document DB is the Sanity Studio for the **Tredyffrin Easttown Historical Society**. It stores primary sources, historical images, curated website essays, TEHS Quarterly citations, and the people, places, and categories that connect them.

## How content is organized

The Studio desk mirrors three sections. Use the same mental model when choosing what to create:

| Section | What belongs here | Document types |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **The Archive** | Historical media you are preserving or transcribing | Primary Sources, Historical Images |
| **The Website** | Modern research pages and Quarterly citation records | Curated Essays, TEHS Quarterly Articles |
| **Taxonomies & Entities** | Shared people, places, properties, businesses, and subjects | Townships, Locations, Persons, Properties, Businesses, Categories |

Read more under [Content model](./content-model/the-archive.mdx).

### When to use which section

- **Archive** — one newspaper clipping, letter, or photograph from the historical record.
- **Website** — a long-form overview for the public site, or bibliographic metadata for a Quarterly article you cite elsewhere.
- **Taxonomies & Entities** — reusable records you link from many archive or website documents (a person, Paoli, Glenhardie Farm, Schools).
